Step 1 of 5 · Onboarding

60 seconds to "future you"

Goal, experience, and a protein target captured in three taps — then we show the projected payoff (a 100kg bench by September) so the goal is imaginable from minute one. Time-to-first-value under a minute is the single biggest retention lever.

Why it matters: Most churn is decided in the first session. We hook with a magic moment and a vision before the user can bounce.

Why this wins

  • Highest-engagement, highest-LTV users (lifters log near-daily)
  • Clear "it's working" signal — PRs, protein, lean-mass trend
  • Rides the GLP-1 wave (muscle-preservation is urgent & underserved)
  • Owns the gym+kitchen seam no incumbent spans
  • Two pillars together = the unique value (the moat)

The risk & the proof point

  • Two pillars at once = more to build than a single-feature wedge

Proof point: Day-30 retention for the strength/recomp cohort beats the 3–10% category baseline (target >20%).

The recommendation: it's not either/or — it's a sequence

Start with Wedge A "Strong & Lean" because it retains the highest-value users and owns the empty gym+kitchen seam. Bake in Wedge B's frictionless-nutrition core (it's half of A anyway), and add Wedge C's intelligence as the v1 "moat" layer once the loop retains and there's enough data to correlate.

v0 · Wedge A loopcapture + unified dashboard + progress story → v1 · Wedge C moatwearables + correlations + "what to do today" + AI coach → v2 · expandendurance · biohacker · coach B2B2C

The bar to clear before moving from v0 → v1: day-30 retention > 20% for the beachhead cohort (vs. the 3–10% category baseline).

The second act

The B2B dual-engine

The one thing no sensor can ever measure is what you ate. Nutrition is every hardware giant's structural blind spot — which is why they all partner for it. Our consumer app's food-AI is also the data flywheel for a licensable Nutrition Intelligence API.

The flywheel

🏋️ Consumer app (Wedge A)
every food photo + every user correction
↓
Proprietary labeled dataset
real meals · real portions · corrected in the wild
↓
Best-in-class food + portion model
owns the hard part: portion estimation
↓
🔌 Nutrition Intelligence API
→ OEMs · telehealth · insurers · giants
↺ funds more growth → more data → better model

Who buys it (and the precedent)

  • Mid-tier OEMs — Coros, Polar, Amazfit, Wahoo, Suunto, white-label
  • Telehealth / GLP-1 platforms — exploding need for protein + food data
  • Insurers & corporate wellness — need logging, won't build it
  • The giants — as occasional customers, or as acquirers
Proven model: Passio.ai sells food-vision SDKs B2B today. Firstbeat licensed its analytics to ~77% of the sports-watch market — then Garmin acquired it. That's the template: become the category's brain, then its acquisition target.
⚠ Reality check: Garmin (Connect+) & Samsung just added native nutrition in 2026 — so own the hard AI/portion layer, not the commodity barcode DB.

The strategic rule

Sequence, don't parallelize. Build the consumer app first so the data flywheel spins; productize the API once the model is differentiated. This turns the project's biggest technical risk (nutrition accuracy) into its biggest strategic asset (a licensable, acquisition-grade moat) — and gives a second revenue path + a clear exit without diluting the consumer focus.
